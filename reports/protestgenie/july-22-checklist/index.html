What to Wear — Bike Riding Gear

Solid instinct — riding gear is designed for the exact injuries lathis cause (blunt force, abrasion). Heat will be uncomfortable but worth it.

  • Full-face helmet LATHI TEARGAS — Protects skull from direct blows. Visor shields eyes from tear gas. Keep visor closed during gas.
  • Riding jacket with armor LATHI — Shoulder, elbow, back armor absorbs lathi hits. Leather/textile slides blows. Mesh jacket if possible (35°C).
  • Riding pants (knee + hip armor) LATHI — Lathis target legs. Knee/shin armor critical when police charge low.
  • Riding gloves with knuckle protection LATHI — Hands are exposed when raising arms to protect face. Gloves save fingers.
  • Riding boots (ankle coverage) LATHI RAIN — Ankle bone is a common lathi target. Boots also keep feet dry in monsoon.
  • Backpack worn on back LATHI TIP — If you have to turn away from a charge, the backpack absorbs spine/back blows. Keep soft items (clothes) on outside.

💡 Heat management: Mesh riding gear breathes. Stay hydrated (ORS in bag). Soak a neck gaiter in water — cools you via evaporation.

Tear Gas — Protection Kit

CS gas (used Jul 20) causes eye burning, coughing, skin irritation. These countermeasures work.

  • N95 / P100 mask TEARGAS — Blocks CS particles. Tuck into helmet chin area if full-face not available.
  • Swimming goggles or ski goggles TEARGAS — Seal around eyes. CS gas burns eyes most severely. Prescription glasses wearers: put goggles OVER them.
  • Bandana / cloth soaked in vinegar or lemon juice TEARGAS CRITICAL — Tie around mouth/nose under mask. Acid neutralizes CS gas alkalinity. Carry small bottle of vinegar in bag.
  • Baking soda + water mixture (small spray bottle) TEARGAS — Spray on exposed skin if gas contacts you. Alkaline neutralizer.
  • Saline eye wash (small bottle) TEARGAS — Flush eyes immediately if gas gets in. Do NOT rub eyes.
  • Full-face helmet (listed above) TEARGAS LATHI — Best single item against gas + lathi. Visor sealed, air filtered through helmet vents.

💡 If gassed without gear: Don't rub. Blink rapidly. Move upwind. Remove contacts immediately. Wash face with baking soda solution. Seek medical if breathing difficulty persists.

During tear gas:

  • Close visor / put on goggles + mask — Immediately upon seeing shells or smoke.
  • Move upwind / perpendicular to wind — Gas cloud drifts. Don't run downwind.
  • Breathe through wet cloth (vinegar) — Neutralizes CS particles in air.
  • Do NOT rub eyes or skin — Flush with saline or water. Pat skin dry.
  • Remove contact lenses immediately — They trap CS particles against cornea.
